What is the integral of (2x^2 + 3x - 1) dx?

Step-by-step Solution:

Step 1: Apply the sum/difference rule for integration. This allows us to integrate each term separately: ∫(2x² + 3x - 1) dx = ∫2x² dx + ∫3x dx - ∫1 dx.

Step 2: Use the power rule for integration, which states ∫axⁿ dx = (a / (n+1)) * xⁿ⁺¹.

∫2x² dx = (2/3)x³
∫3x dx = (3/2)x²
∫1 dx = x

Step 3: Combine the results and add the constant of integration, C, as this is an indefinite integral.

Final Answer: (2/3)x³ + (3/2)x² - x + C
